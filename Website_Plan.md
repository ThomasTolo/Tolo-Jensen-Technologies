I actually think this website is an opportunity to build a **brand**, not just a portfolio.

Looking at the inspiration from Baltic AI, I like the **structure** (large sections, storytelling, premium feeling), but I wouldn't copy the aesthetic. Instead, I'd lean toward something that feels like a blend of:

- Linear (clean motion)
    
- Apple Developer
    
- Vercel
    
- Stripe
    
- Framer
    
- with Scandinavian minimalism
    

Since you're **Tolo Jensen Development**, your website should communicate:

> _"A Norwegian software engineer building polished software experiences—from student life to entertainment."_

Not "I'm a student with some projects."

Instead, the feeling should be:

> "This is a young software company that's going somewhere."

# Language

Since you're Norwegian but may attract international visitors:

- Detect the browser language on first visit.
    
- Default to **Norwegian** for visitors from Norway.
    
- Default to **English** otherwise.
    
- Allow instant switching between the two languages, remembering the user's preference.
    

---

# Animation library

I would use:

- **Framer Motion** for page transitions, element reveals, counters, and micro-interactions.
    
- **GSAP + ScrollTrigger** for the immersive scroll experience where sections pin, slide, scale, blur, and reveal one another. This gives you much finer control than standard scroll animations.
    
- CSS transitions for lightweight hover effects to keep performance high.
    

---

# Extra touches

To make the site memorable without feeling gimmicky:

- A subtle animated gradient or particle background that reacts gently to scrolling.
    
- Soft parallax effects on device mockups and illustrations.
    
- Cursor effects only on desktop (small magnetic buttons, slight hover attraction).
    
- Animated phone mockups showing live previews of Kollekt.
    
- Loading screen with the **Tolo Jensen Development** logo morphing into the homepage.
    
- A "Now Building" banner highlighting your current focus (for example, Kollekt or the next game project).
    
- Smooth page transitions even when navigating between routes, so the whole site feels like one continuous experience.

## Overall vision

The goal shouldn't be to say _"here are my projects."_

It should feel like visitors are stepping into your studio.

Each scroll reveals another part of your journey:

1. **Who I am** — establish the brand.
    
2. **How I think** — your philosophy and approach.
    
3. **What I've built** — showcase Kollekt and other work.
    
4. **What's coming next** — drinking games, escape room experiences, and future products.
    
5. **Play something** — interactive daily games that encourage repeat visits.
    
6. **Let's build together** — contact and company information.
    

Done well, the site won't feel like a portfolio—it will feel like the digital headquarters of **Tolo Jensen Development**, reflecting both your identity as a software engineering student and your ambition to grow into a product-focused software company.
